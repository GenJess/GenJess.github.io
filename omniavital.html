Problem

Supplements have a retention problem.

Most supplement brands operate a broken model: acquire customers cheaply, ship product, watch them churn. No personalization, no engagement, no reason to come back.

📦
Dropshipping ≠ wellness
The standard model: white-label product, Facebook ad, one-time purchase. Zero relationship with the customer. No understanding of what they actually need.
🤷
Generic recommendations
"Take Vitamin D." Great — how much? When? With what? The gap between what a product page says and what a pharmacist would tell you is enormous.
📉
85%+ churn rate
Most supplement subscriptions die after one or two orders. No feedback loop, no adaptation, no reason for the customer to believe it's working.
🏥
No biometric feedback loop
Apple Watch tracks everything. None of it connects to your supplement stack. OmniaVital closes that loop — recommendations adapt to what your body is actually showing.
System Design

Three layers, one experience.

Voice onboarding → RAG recommendation → retention agent. Each layer reinforces the next.

1
Voice Onboarding — "OV"
ElevenLabs conversational AI handles intake. Asks about health goals, existing supplements, sleep, stress, diet. Natural conversation, not a form. Sub-500ms latency. Custom persona with supplement domain knowledge baked in. Outputs structured user profile for the recommendation engine.
ElevenLabs Conversational AI
2
RAG Recommendation Engine
User profile hits the RAG pipeline: query → vector search across supplement knowledge base (NIH studies, pharmacist-reviewed protocols, interaction warnings) → ranked results → structured recommendation with dosage, timing, and rationale. Every recommendation is sourced and explainable.
RAG · Vector DB · Pharmacist-Reviewed Sources
3
Post-Purchase Dashboard
Track supplements taken, biometrics from Apple Watch (sleep, HRV, activity), and user-reported outcomes. Visual trends over time. The dashboard closes the feedback loop — the user can see whether what they're taking is working.
HealthKit · Biometric Tracking
4
Behavioral Retention Agent
Background agent monitors adherence, biometric signals, and time-since-last-engagement. Surfaces nudges, check-ins, and recommendation updates at the right moment. Designed to genuinely help the user — but the engagement mechanics also drive re-subscription and upsell. Both are true simultaneously.
Retention Agent · Behavioral Triggers
5
Community Layer
Privacy-first community matching: users see color-gradient representations of other users' supplement stacks — similar profiles get matched without exposing personal health data. Drives social proof and retention without sacrificing privacy.
Privacy-First · Color-Gradient Matching
Evaluation

I tested it against a pharmacist.

Most AI wellness products never evaluate recommendation quality against domain experts. I did.

30 Test cases
ASR Failure modes tracked
Latency Tradeoffs logged
Each test case: a user profile (health goals, existing supplements, conditions) fed to the RAG pipeline, generating a recommendation. A pharmacist reviewed each recommendation for accuracy, safety, and completeness. Failure modes were categorized: missing interactions, incorrect dosing, irrelevant recommendations. Results drove iteration on the retrieval strategy and prompt design — not just vibes.
Why this matters: Evaluating an LLM system against a domain expert baseline is what separates a product from a demo. Most AI wellness apps ship without any ground-truth validation. The pharmacist review gave OmniaVital a real signal — and a benchmark to beat as the system improves.
Design Decisions

The calls that shaped the product.

Why voice-first onboarding instead of a form?
Forms kill conversion in wellness. The intake process is personal — health goals, stress levels, sleep quality. A conversation feels appropriate; a form feels clinical. ElevenLabs' latency is now low enough that the voice agent doesn't feel like a bot. The output is also richer — users say more in conversation than they type in boxes.
Why RAG instead of a fine-tuned model?
Supplement science changes. Studies get updated. New interactions get discovered. A fine-tuned model bakes in a snapshot of knowledge at training time. RAG lets me update the knowledge base without retraining. It also makes recommendations explainable — I can show the user exactly which study or protocol a recommendation came from.
Why HealthKit instead of manual biometric entry?
Manual entry has zero retention. HealthKit already has the data — the user just needs to authorize it. Sleep, HRV, activity, weight — all available passively. Passive data collection is the only kind that works at scale for a health product.
Why color-gradient matching instead of profile matching for community?
Health data is deeply personal. Users won't share their supplement stack publicly. But they will engage with an abstracted representation that shows similarity without exposure. The color gradient is a privacy-preserving proxy for "people like you are doing X" — the most powerful wellness motivator there is.
